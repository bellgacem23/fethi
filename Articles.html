  1. CONVERGENT APPROACHES FOR THE DIRICHLET MONGE-AMPÈRE PROBLEM

    2. In this article, I work with Imen Hajri. We introduce and study three numerical methods for the Dirichlet Monge Ampère equation in two dimensions.
    The approaches consist of considering new equivalent problems. The latter are discretized by a large stencil, finite difference discretization and monotonic schemes are obtained. Therefore, we apply the Barles-Souganidis theory to prove the convergence of the schemes and the method of damped Newtons is used to calculate the solutions of the schemes. Finally, some numerical results are illustrated.

  2. Convergence of Numerical Approximations to Non-linear Continuity Equations with Rough Force Fields

    We demonstrate quantitative regularity estimates for solutions of nonlinear continuity equations and their numerical approximations discretized on Cartesian grids when advected by an approximate force field.
    This allows us to find the optimal regularity known for the linear transport equations but also to obtain the convergence of a wide range of numerical schemes. Our demonstration is based on a new estimation of the commutator, quantifying and extending to the nonlinear case the approach of the theory of renormalized solutions.
